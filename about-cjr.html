About the Center for Jain Studies (CJS)

Jainism is one of the oldest living culture and tradition of India. The Centre for Jain Studies has been established at Devi Ahilya Vishwavidyalaya, Indore, under the Pradhan Mantri Jan Vikas Karyakram (PMJVK), Ministry of Minority Affairs, Government of India, New Delhi, with the objective of providing an academic platform dedicated to the preservation, study, research, and dissemination of Jain philosophy, culture, literature, and allied subjects. It will also serve as a Nodal Centre for various academic, cultural and social activities in line with Jain traditions and heritage.

The Centre aims to advance the study, preservation, and promotion of Jain philosophy, heritage, and culture, as an integral part of Indian knowledge traditions. It would engage in rigorous teaching, training, research, and advanced studies in Jainism and allied disciplines while establishing interdisciplinary linkages and collaborating with national and global scholars and institutions. Through outreach, dialogue, publications, and community engagement, the Centre seeks to inspire individuals and societies to embrace values of non-violence, compassion, and sustainability—cultivating peace, harmony, and meaningful living.

Vision

To preserve, promote and enhance Jain Philosophy, Heritage and Culture as an integral part of Indian knowledge traditions for peace, sustainability and harmonious living.

Mission

  • To advance the study, preservation, and dissemination of Jain philosophy, heritage, and culture, as an integral part of Indian knowledge traditions.
  • To promotes rigorous teaching, training, research, and advanced studies in Jainism and allied disciplines while establishing interdisciplinary linkages and collaborating with national and global scholars and institutions.
  • To inspire individuals and societies to embrace values of non-violence, compassion, and sustainability - cultivating peace, harmony, and meaningful living through outreach, dialogue, publications, and community engagement.

Thrust Areas

Preserve and Promote Jain Heritage and Culture

Offer academic programs, courses, publishing research, and host national and international academic activities for promoting the heritage and understanding of Jainism.

Contribute to Indian Knowledge System

Research and studies for contribution of Jain Literature in the fields of art, culture, philosophy, linguistics, literature, spirituality, education, humanities, grammar, ayurveda, science, mathematics, technology, astrology, architecture, astronomy, environment etc., as an integral part of Indian Knowledge System.

Foster Interdisciplinary Research

Facilitate interdisciplinary research, bringing together scholars from various fields to explore Jainism's multifaceted contribution, validation of Jain practices such as Ahimsa, Aahar, Mantra etc.

Enhance Global Understanding

Collaborate with national and international scholars and institutions to enhance and disseminate knowledge about Jainism globally.

Support Community Engagement

Serve as a hub for community engagement, providing a platform for dialogue and collaboration between scholars, practitioners and the general public.

Objectives

  1. To promote teaching, research, and advanced studies in Jainism, Jain philosophy, culture, history, literature, Jain Yoga and other allied subjects.
  2. To preserve, document and enhance Knowledge Resources on Jainism for academic and cultural enrichment.
  3. To study the contribution of Jain Literature in different domains of Indian Knowledge System.
  4. To develop interdisciplinary linkages connecting Jain philosophy with modern fields such as ethics, ecology, peace studies, management, and wellness.
  5. To provide Vocational Training – Lifestyle, Health, Craft, Yoga, other skills.
  6. To collaborate with national and global scholars and centres / institutions of Jain Studies and Interdisciplinary areas.
  7. To organize Community Outreach activities and to publish scholarly articles, newsletters, journals, books etc.
  8. To provide an international platform for the advanced studies of Jainism in various contexts.
  9. To conduct seminars, workshops, conferences, academic and extension activities for spreading awareness about Jain values, philosophy, knowledge, practices and their practical applications.
  10. To promote practices of Jain Yoga, meditation, and mindfulness for holistic development and well-being.
